# Squat Form Analysis Guide

## Key Joint Angles

- **Hip angle at bottom**: 70-110 degrees indicates adequate depth
- **Knee angle at bottom**: 80-100 degrees for full ROM
- **Knee tracking**: Knees should track over toes, not collapse inward (valgus > 15 degrees is a fault)
- **Torso angle**: 30-45 degrees forward lean is normal for back squat

## Common Mistakes

1. **Knee valgus**: Knees cave inward — cue "push knees out"
2. **Insufficient depth**: Thighs not parallel — cue "sit deeper, chest up"
3. **Excessive forward lean**: Torso > 50 degrees — cue "brace core, keep chest up"
4. **Heel rise**: Weight on toes — cue "drive through heels"

## Rep Counting FSM

- **Eccentric phase**: Hip angle decreasing below 120 degrees
- **Bottom position**: Hip angle < 100 degrees AND knee angle < 100 degrees
- **Concentric phase**: Hip angle increasing from bottom
- **Rep complete**: Return to standing (hip angle > 160 degrees)
